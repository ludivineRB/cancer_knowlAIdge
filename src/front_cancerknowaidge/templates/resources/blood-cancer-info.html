Blood Cancer Information

Learn about the different types of blood cancers, symptoms, treatment approaches, and support for patients and families.

What is Blood Cancer?

Blood cancer affects the production and function of blood cells. It typically starts in the bone marrow where blood is produced, leading to abnormal growth of blood cells that interfere with normal blood functions.

Key Facts

  • • Includes leukemia, lymphoma, and myeloma
  • • Affects people of all ages
  • • Early diagnosis improves treatment outcomes
  • • Advances in immunotherapy offer new hope

Types of Blood Cancer

Leukemia

Cancer of the blood-forming tissues, leading to excessive production of abnormal white blood cells

Lymphoma

Affects the lymphatic system, impairing the body's ability to fight infection

Myeloma

Cancer of the plasma cells, which produce antibodies to fight infections

Symptoms of Blood Cancer

Symptoms may vary depending on the type of blood cancer, but common signs include:

Fatigue

Persistent tiredness or weakness due to low red blood cell counts (anemia)

Frequent Infections

Weakened immune system leading to repeated or severe infections

Easy Bruising & Bleeding

Low platelet count causes bleeding gums, nosebleeds, or easy bruising

Treatment Options

Treatment depends on the type of blood cancer, its stage, and the patient’s overall health.

Standard Treatments

Chemotherapy

Destroys rapidly dividing cancer cells; used alone or with other therapies

Radiation Therapy

Targets specific areas affected by cancer, often in lymphomas

Stem Cell Transplant

Replaces diseased bone marrow with healthy stem cells

Advanced Treatments

Targeted Therapy

Blocks specific molecules involved in cancer growth

Immunotherapy

Stimulates the immune system to attack cancer cells (e.g., CAR T-cell therapy)

Clinical Trials

Access to experimental therapies and novel treatment approaches

Support & Resources

Support services are essential for patients and caregivers throughout the cancer journey.

Counseling Services

Emotional and mental health support for patients and families

Financial Assistance
