Prostate Cancer Information

Comprehensive information about prostate cancer types, screening, treatment options, and support resources.

Understanding Prostate Cancer

Prostate cancer occurs when cells in the prostate gland grow uncontrollably. It is the second most common cancer in men worldwide. Early detection can significantly improve treatment outcomes and survival rates.

Key Statistics

  • • 1 in 8 men will be diagnosed with prostate cancer in their lifetime
  • • 5-year survival rate is nearly 100% for localized cases
  • • Most common in men over 65
  • • Risk increases with family history and ethnicity

Types of Prostate Cancer

Adenocarcinoma

The most common type, originating in gland cells of the prostate.

Small Cell Carcinoma

A rare, aggressive form of prostate cancer.

Squamous Cell Carcinoma

Another rare type that develops in flat cells of the prostate.

Transitional Cell Carcinoma

Begins in the urethra and spreads to the prostate.

Screening & Early Detection

Screening helps detect prostate cancer at an early, more treatable stage.

PSA Test

Measures prostate-specific antigen levels in the blood to detect abnormalities.

Recommended:

  • • Ages 50-69: Discuss screening with doctor
  • • High risk: Start earlier at age 45

Digital Rectal Exam (DRE)

A physical exam to check the prostate for lumps or abnormalities.

Usage:

  • • Often combined with PSA test
  • • Quick and minimally invasive

Prostate Biopsy

Confirms the diagnosis by analyzing prostate tissue under a microscope.

When needed:

  • • Abnormal PSA or DRE results
  • • Helps determine cancer aggressiveness

Treatment Options

Treatment depends on cancer stage, age, and overall health.

Local Treatments

Surgery

Radical Prostatectomy

Removal of the prostate gland and surrounding tissues.

Radiation Therapy

  • • External beam radiation
  • • Brachytherapy (internal radiation)
  • • Often used for localized cancer

Systemic Treatments

Hormone Therapy

  • • Lowers androgen levels to slow cancer growth
  • • LHRH agonists or antagonists

Chemotherapy

  • • Used for advanced prostate cancer
  • • Often combined with hormone therapy

Targeted Therapy

  • • Targets genetic mutations (e.g., PARP inhibitors)
