Managing Cancer-Related Fatigue

Comprehensive strategies to understand, manage, and cope with cancer-related fatigue during and after treatment.

Understanding Cancer Fatigue

Cancer-related fatigue is different from normal tiredness. It's a persistent, overwhelming exhaustion that doesn't improve with rest and can significantly impact daily activities and quality of life.

Key Characteristics

  • • More severe than normal tiredness
  • • Not relieved by sleep or rest
  • • Interferes with daily activities
  • • May persist after treatment ends
  • • Affects physical, emotional, and mental energy

Causes of Cancer Fatigue

Treatment-Related

  • • Chemotherapy side effects
  • • Radiation therapy
  • • Surgery and recovery
  • • Immunotherapy

Cancer-Related

  • • Tumor burden on the body
  • • Anemia from cancer or treatment
  • • Metabolic changes
  • • Immune system activation

Secondary Factors

  • • Sleep disturbances
  • • Poor nutrition
  • • Dehydration
  • • Emotional stress

Energy Conservation Strategies

Learn how to manage your energy more effectively and prioritize activities that matter most.

Plan & Prioritize

  • • Make daily activity lists
  • • Prioritize most important tasks
  • • Schedule activities during peak energy times
  • • Break large tasks into smaller steps
  • • Delegate when possible

Pace Yourself

  • • Take frequent rest breaks
  • • Alternate activity with rest
  • • Avoid rushing through tasks
  • • Listen to your body's signals
  • • Stop before becoming exhausted

Modify Activities

  • • Sit while doing tasks when possible
  • • Use labor-saving devices
  • • Organize workspace efficiently
  • • Prepare meals in advance
  • • Accept help from others

Exercise & Physical Activity

Gentle exercise can actually help reduce fatigue and improve energy levels over time.

Benefits of Exercise

  • Improves cardiovascular fitness
  • Maintains muscle strength
  • Enhances mood and reduces anxiety
  • Improves sleep quality
  • Reduces cancer-related fatigue
  • Boosts immune function

Recommended Activities

Low-Impact Aerobic

  • • Walking (start with 5-10 minutes)
  • • Swimming or water aerobics
  • • Stationary cycling
  • • Chair exercises

Strength Training

  • • Light weights or resistance bands
  • • Bodyweight exercises
  • • 2-3 times per week
  • • Focus on major muscle groups

Flexibility & Balance

  • • Gentle yoga or tai chi
  • • Stretching exercises
  • • Balance training
  • • Deep breathing exercises

Exercise Guidelines

  • • Start slowly and gradually increase
  • • Exercise when energy levels are highest
  • • Stop if you feel dizzy or short of breath
  • • Consult your healthcare team before starting

Sleep & Rest Strategies

Quality sleep is essential for managing fatigue and supporting your body's healing process.

Sleep Hygiene Tips

Create a Sleep Schedule

  • • Go to bed and wake up at the same time daily
  • • Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep per night
  • • Maintain schedule even on weekends

Optimize Sleep Environment

  • • Keep bedroom cool, dark, and quiet
  • • Use comfortable mattress and pillows
  • • Remove electronic devices

Pre-Sleep Routine

  • • Develop relaxing bedtime routine
  • • Avoid caffeine 6 hours before bed
  • • Try warm bath or gentle stretching

Strategic Napping

Napping Guidelines

  • • Keep naps short (20-30 minutes)
  • • Nap before 3 PM to avoid nighttime sleep issues
  • • Create a comfortable nap environment
  • • Set an alarm to prevent oversleeping
  • • Don't feel guilty about needing rest

Rest vs. Sleep

Active Rest
  • • Gentle stretching or yoga
  • • Meditation or deep breathing
  • • Listening to calming music
  • • Light reading
Passive Rest
  • • Lying down with eyes closed
  • • Sitting quietly in comfortable chair
  • • Progressive muscle relaxation
  • • Guided imagery

When to Contact Your Care Team

Severe fatigue may indicate other medical issues that need attention.

Contact Your Team If:

  • • Fatigue suddenly worsens
  • • Unable to perform daily activities
  • • Fatigue accompanied by fever
  • • Shortness of breath with minimal activity
  • • Persistent insomnia
  • • Signs of depression

Possible Interventions:

  • • Blood tests to check for anemia
  • • Medication adjustments
  • • Nutritional supplements
  • • Sleep study referral
  • • Physical therapy consultation
  • • Mental health support
