Chemotherapy Treatment Guide

Comprehensive information about chemotherapy treatment, what to expect, and how to manage side effects.

How Chemotherapy Works

Chemotherapy uses powerful medications to destroy cancer cells or slow their growth. These drugs work by targeting rapidly dividing cells, which is a characteristic of cancer cells.

Cell Cycle Disruption

Interferes with cancer cell division and DNA replication

Systemic Treatment

Travels through bloodstream to reach cancer cells throughout the body

Multiple Mechanisms

Different drugs work in various ways to maximize effectiveness

Types of Chemotherapy

  • Alkylating agents: Damage DNA directly
  • Antimetabolites: Interfere with DNA and RNA synthesis
  • Plant alkaloids: Stop cell division
  • Topoisomerase inhibitors: Block DNA repair

Managing Side Effects

Proactive strategies to minimize and manage chemotherapy side effects.

Common Side Effects

Nausea and Vomiting

Most common side effect, usually preventable with medication

  • • Anti-nausea medications before treatment
  • • Dietary modifications
  • • Ginger and acupuncture may help

Fatigue

Extreme tiredness that doesn't improve with rest

  • • Energy conservation techniques
  • • Light exercise as tolerated
  • • Adequate sleep and nutrition

Hair Loss

Usually temporary, begins 2-3 weeks after treatment

  • • Scalp cooling caps may help
  • • Wig fitting before hair loss
  • • Gentle hair care products

Prevention Strategies

Proactive Care Plan

Before Treatment
  • • Dental checkup and cleaning
  • • Nutritional assessment
  • • Vaccination updates
  • • Baseline blood work
During Treatment
  • • Regular blood monitoring
  • • Infection prevention measures
  • • Hydration maintenance
  • • Symptom tracking
Between Cycles
  • • Recovery time for normal cells
  • • Nutritional support
  • • Gentle physical activity
  • • Emotional support

When to Contact Your Care Team

Call Immediately If:

  • • Fever over 100.4°F (38°C)
  • • Signs of infection
  • • Severe nausea preventing fluid intake
  • • Difficulty breathing
  • • Unusual bleeding or bruising

Contact Within 24 Hours:

  • • Persistent vomiting
  • • Severe diarrhea or constipation
  • • Mouth sores preventing eating
  • • Severe fatigue or weakness
  • • New or worsening symptoms

24/7 Emergency Line: (555) 911-CARE
