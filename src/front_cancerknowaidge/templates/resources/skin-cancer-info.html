Skin Cancer Information

Explore the different types of skin cancer, recognize early warning signs, and learn about treatment options and prevention tips.

What is Skin Cancer?

Skin cancer is the uncontrolled growth of abnormal skin cells, often caused by damage from ultraviolet (UV) radiation. Early detection and treatment are crucial for successful outcomes.

Key Facts

  • • Most common cancer worldwide
  • • Caused primarily by UV exposure
  • • Early detection saves lives
  • • Preventable with proper sun protection

Types of Skin Cancer

Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC)

The most common type; grows slowly and rarely spreads

Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC)

May grow deeper and spread if untreated

Melanoma

Less common but more dangerous; can spread rapidly

Symptoms of Skin Cancer

Be aware of changes in your skin and consult a doctor if you notice:

New Growths

Unusual moles, bumps, or patches that appear or change

Non-Healing Sores

Sores that bleed, ooze, or don’t heal over several weeks

Changes in Moles

Look for asymmetry, irregular borders, color changes, or size increase

Treatment Options

Treatment varies by cancer type and stage but often includes:

Standard Treatments

Surgical Removal

Excision or Mohs surgery to remove cancerous tissue

Cryotherapy

Freezing precancerous or small cancer cells with liquid nitrogen

Topical Treatments

Medicines applied directly to the skin for superficial cancers

Advanced Treatments

Radiation Therapy

Used for cancers in hard-to-reach areas or advanced stages

Immunotherapy

Helps the immune system recognize and attack melanoma cells

Targeted Therapy

For melanomas with specific genetic mutations (e.g., BRAF inhibitors)

Prevention & Awareness

Simple lifestyle changes can significantly reduce your risk of skin cancer:

Use Sunscreen

Apply broad-spectrum SPF 30+ sunscreen daily

Avoid Tanning Beds

Ultraviolet light from tanning beds increases cancer risk

Regular Skin Checks

Examine your skin monthly and see a dermatologist annually
