Lung Cancer Information

Essential information about lung cancer types, screening, treatment approaches, and support services.

Understanding Lung Cancer

Lung cancer occurs when abnormal cells grow uncontrollably in the lung tissues. It is the leading cause of cancer death worldwide. Early detection greatly improves survival rates.

Key Statistics

  • • Most common cancer worldwide
  • • Leading cause of cancer deaths in both men and women
  • • Often diagnosed in people over 65
  • • Smoking is the primary risk factor

Types of Lung Cancer

Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC)

Most common type (85%), includes adenocarcinoma, squamous cell carcinoma, and large cell carcinoma

Small Cell Lung Cancer (SCLC)

Less common but more aggressive, often linked to smoking

Lung Carcinoid Tumors

Rare, grows slower and less likely to spread

Screening & Early Detection

Early screening helps identify lung cancer before symptoms develop, especially in high-risk individuals.

Low-Dose CT Scan

Recommended for high-risk individuals (age 50-80, heavy smokers or recent quitters)

Recognizing Symptoms

Persistent cough, chest pain, shortness of breath, or coughing up blood should prompt medical evaluation

Risk Assessment

Doctors evaluate smoking history, family history, and occupational exposures to assess screening needs

Treatment Options

Treatments depend on the type, stage, and patient health. A multidisciplinary approach is often used.

Local Treatments

Surgery

Lobectomy

Removal of a lung lobe containing the tumor

Pneumonectomy

Removal of an entire lung in advanced cases

Segmentectomy/Wedge Resection

Removal of smaller sections in early-stage cancer

Radiation Therapy

  • • Often used when surgery isn’t possible
  • • Stereotactic body radiation (SBRT) for small tumors
  • • May be combined with chemotherapy

Systemic Treatments

Chemotherapy

  • • Kills cancer cells throughout the body
  • • May be used before or after surgery
  • • Standard for SCLC and advanced NSCLC

Targeted Therapy

  • • For cancers with specific gene mutations (EGFR, ALK)
  • • Less toxic than chemotherapy
  • • Oral medications often used

Immunotherapy

  • • Helps immune system attack cancer
  • • PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitors (e.g., Keytruda)
  • • Effective for many advanced NSCLC cases
