Risk Factors & Prevention

Understanding risk factors and prevention strategies for different cancer types.

Common Risk Factors

Lifestyle Factors

  • • Tobacco use and exposure to secondhand smoke
  • • Excessive alcohol consumption
  • • Poor diet and lack of physical activity
  • • Obesity and maintaining unhealthy weight
  • • Excessive sun exposure and UV radiation

Environmental & Occupational

  • • Exposure to carcinogenic chemicals
  • • Radiation exposure from medical procedures
  • • Asbestos and other workplace hazards
  • • Air pollution and environmental toxins
  • • Certain infections (HPV, Hepatitis B/C)

Prevention Strategies

Healthy Lifestyle Choices

  • • Maintain a healthy diet rich in fruits and vegetables
  • • Exercise regularly and maintain healthy weight
  • • Avoid tobacco products and limit alcohol
  • • Practice sun safety and use sunscreen
  • • Get recommended vaccinations (HPV, Hepatitis B)

Regular Screening

  • • Follow recommended screening guidelines
  • • Mammograms for breast cancer detection
  • • Colonoscopy for colorectal cancer screening
  • • Pap tests and HPV testing for cervical cancer
  • • Discuss genetic testing if family history present

Warning Signs to Watch For

Early detection saves lives. Know the warning signs and when to contact your healthcare provider.

General Warning Signs

  • Unexplained weight loss or gain
  • Persistent fatigue or weakness
  • Changes in bowel or bladder habits
  • Unusual bleeding or discharge
  • Lumps or thickening in breast or elsewhere
  • Persistent cough or hoarseness
  • Changes in skin moles or lesions
  • Difficulty swallowing or indigestion

Important: These symptoms can have many causes other than cancer. However, if you experience any of these signs persistently for more than two weeks, contact your healthcare provider for evaluation.
