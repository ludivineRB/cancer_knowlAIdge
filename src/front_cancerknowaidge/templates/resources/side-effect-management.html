Emotional & Mental Health Support

Managing the emotional impact of cancer treatment is just as important as managing physical side effects.

Common Emotional Responses

Anxiety & Fear

Worry about treatment outcomes, future health, and impact on family is normal.

  • • Relaxation and breathing techniques
  • • Mindfulness and meditation practices
  • • Professional counseling support

Depression & Sadness

Feeling sad, hopeless, or losing interest in activities you once enjoyed.

  • • Stay connected with loved ones
  • • Maintain routine activities when possible
  • • Consider antidepressant medication if needed

Coping Strategies

Build Your Support Network

  • • Join cancer support groups
  • • Stay connected with family and friends
  • • Consider online communities
  • • Work with social workers and counselors

Stress Management

  • • Practice deep breathing exercises
  • • Try progressive muscle relaxation
  • • Engage in gentle physical activity
  • • Pursue hobbies and creative activities

When to Contact Your Care Team

Know when side effects require immediate medical attention.

Call Immediately If You Experience:

  • Fever over 100.4°F (38°C)
  • Severe nausea preventing fluid intake
  • Difficulty breathing or chest pain
  • Signs of infection (redness, swelling, pus)
  • Unusual bleeding or bruising
  • Severe diarrhea or constipation
  • Severe skin reactions or rash
  • Thoughts of self-harm

Emergency Contact: (555) 911-CARE - Available 24/7 for urgent concerns

Don't hesitate to call if you're unsure whether a symptom requires immediate attention. Our team is here to help you navigate any concerns.
